Shenzhen is often the first city foreigners see in mainland China — it's modern, visa-friendly for many transit travelers, and sits right next to Hong Kong. Here is a **realistic 3-day plan** that balances iconic sights with food and rest.

## Day 1: Futian & Civic Center

Start at **Shenzhen Museum** (free, English labels) to understand how a fishing village became a tech hub. Walk to **Ping An Finance Centre** area for skyline photos.

**Stay:** Futian or Nanshan — both have metro access and international hotels.

## Day 2: OCT-LOFT & Sea World

Morning at **OCT-LOFT** (art district, cafés). Afternoon at **Sea World** in Shekou — expat-friendly dining and waterfront.

Take Metro Line 2; taxis are cheap but use DiDi with English interface after linking a card.

## Day 3: Window of the World or Day Trip Prep

If you want a theme park lite experience, **Window of the World** packs global landmarks into one day. Alternatively, use Day 3 to rest and prep a **Hong Kong day trip** (30 min high-speed rail from Futian).
