Getting online in China is the #1 practical worry for new visitors. A **China-capable eSIM** bought before departure beats airport SIM counters most of the time.

## What you need

- Data that works on **China Mobile / China Unicom** networks
- Enough GB for maps + messaging (5–10 GB / week is typical)
- Clarity on whether **Google/WhatsApp/Instagram** work (often they don't without extra setup)

## Recommended approach

1. Buy a **travel eSIM** that lists mainland China explicitly
2. Install before takeoff; activate on landing
3. Keep WeChat as your main chat app while in China

## Physical SIM vs eSIM

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| eSIM | No store visit, dual-SIM friendly | Phone must support eSIM |
| Airport SIM | Staff can help install | Queues, passport copy, sometimes overpriced |
| Roaming | Simple | Expensive, may block in China |
